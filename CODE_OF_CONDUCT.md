# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

CLI Command Book is a community reference, and we want it to be a welcoming place for anyone who wants to contribute - whether that's fixing a typo or adding a whole new section. We pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of experience level, background, or identity.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that helps create a positive environment:

- Being respectful and considerate in comments, issues, and pull requests
- Giving constructive feedback, and receiving it gracefully
- Focusing disagreements on the content/code, not the person
- Being patient with newer contributors

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, insults, or derogatory comments of any kind
- Personal or political attacks
- Publishing others' private information without consent
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces (issues, pull requests, discussions) and when representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported by opening an issue or contacting the maintainer directly. All complaints will be reviewed and result in a response appropriate to the circumstances, which may include a warning, temporary ban, or permanent ban from the project.

Maintainers have the right to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that don't align with this Code of Conduct.
